Marie Skłodowska Curie

The first ever person in history to be honored with two Nobel prizes!

Here is a timeline of Madame Curie's life

  • 1867 - Marie Skłodowska was born on November 7th in Poland, Russian Empire.
  • 1883 - Attended a gymnasium for girls, from which she graduated on 12 June with a gold medal.
  • 1891 - At the age of 24, she followed her older sister Bronisława to study in Paris, where she earned her higher degrees and conducted her subsequent scientific work.
  • 1893 - Awarded a degree in physics and began work in an industrial laboratory of Professor Gabriel Lippmann.
  • 1895 - Marries her love, Professor Pierre Curie in Sceaux.
  • 1897 - Becomes a mother with her first child, Irene, and Curie began teaching at the École Normale Supérieure.
  • 1898 - Curies announced the existence of a second element, which they named "radium", from the Latin word for "ray".
  • 1900 - Became the first woman faculty member at the École Normale Supérieure and her husband joined the faculty of the University of Paris.
  • 1903 - Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences awarded Pierre Curie, Marie Curie, and Henri Becquerel the Nobel Prize in Physics.
  • 1911 - Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences honoured her a second time, with the 1911 Nobel Prize in Chemistry.
  • 1930 - elected to the International Atomic Weights Committee, on which she served until her death.
  • 1934 - Died at the Sancellemoz sanatorium in Passy, Haute-Savoie, from aplastic anaemia believed to have been contracted from her long-term exposure to radiation.
  • 1995 - Reburied in the Pantheon, France’s national mausoleum, becoming the first women accorded that honor on her own merit.
